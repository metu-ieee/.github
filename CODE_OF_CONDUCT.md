# METU IEEE Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge
We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our Standards
Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment for our community include:
* Demonstrating empathy and kindness toward other students.
* Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences.
* Giving and gracefully accepting constructive code review feedback.
* Focusing on what is best not just for us as individuals, but for the overall student society.

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:
* The use of sexualized language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances of any kind.
* Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks.
* Public or private harassment.
* "Gatekeeping" or mocking a member for their level of technical experience. 
* Publishing others' private information, such as a physical or email address, without explicit permission.

## Enforcement Responsibilities
The `cs-maintainers` and the METU IEEE CS Executive Board are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned to this Code of Conduct, and will communicate reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate.

## Scope
This Code of Conduct applies within all METU IEEE organization spaces (GitHub, Discord, WhatsApp) and also applies when an individual is officially representing the community in public spaces (such as Hackathons or university events).

## Enforcement
Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported to the community leaders responsible for enforcement by contacting the current IEEE CS President or the Executive Board. All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.
